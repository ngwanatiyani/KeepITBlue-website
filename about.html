About KeepITBlue

Our History

Founded in 2024 by a passionate group of environmental advocates, KeepITBlue began as a grassroots initiative focused on addressing the growing threat of ocean pollution. Recognizing the critical need for action, the founding members—including Tiyani Ngwana, Amanda Msutu, Samukelo Ndela, Sikelela Ntingi, Zusiphe Mvovo, Abongile Phandle, environmentalists, and community leaders—came together to create an organization dedicated to preserving marine ecosystems and raising public awareness about the impacts of ocean pollution.

Our first major project was a local beach cleanup in Cape Town, which saw overwhelming support from volunteers and local businesses. The success of this initiative inspired us to expand our reach, organizing educational workshops, advocacy campaigns, and community-driven environmental projects.

Our Mission

To protect our oceans and beaches through community engagement, education, and innovative solutions that foster a sustainable future.

Our Vision

A world where our oceans are clean, healthy, and thriving, benefiting all forms of life and ensuring a sustainable future for generations to come.

Growth and Milestones:

  • 2025: Launched the Blue Program, empowering local communities to take ownership of their coastlines through monthly cleanups and monitoring efforts.
  • 2026: Expanded operations internationally, establishing partnerships with environmental organizations in countries such as Australia, Canada, and the United Kingdom to tackle ocean pollution globally.
  • 2027: Introduced an innovative marine debris tracking system in collaboration with leading marine scientists, enabling us to monitor waste patterns and target high-risk areas for intervention.
  • 2028: Opened our first Ocean Education Center in Cape Town, where students, tourists, and the public can learn about ocean ecosystems, conservation strategies, and how individuals can make a tangible impact on marine health.
